What I See Underneath the Job Description

Four operational realities most candidates miss.

Anyone can read a job description. Fewer take the time to understand the business underneath it. These are the realities I have taken from your post and your website — and they shape every commitment that follows.

i.

You're not running one business. You're running four.

UK new-build sales. Dubai off-plan. CasaCity lettings. Cross-sell into mortgages, solicitors, valuations, snagging, and renovations. Each has its own pipeline, paperwork rhythm, and stakeholders. The seams between them are exactly where deals leak. I see those seams. I close them.

ii.

Speed and accuracy are in tension — and you need both.

A property consultancy moves at the pace of its slowest stakeholder: a quiet solicitor, a late stock list, a missing CRM phone number. The role exists to close those gaps before they become escalations. That is the work I do best.

iii.

The CEO's time is your scarcest resource.

Your post makes this explicit: nothing should fall through the cracks, but the CEO shouldn't be the one watching for them either. The job is to be a smart filter. I filter ruthlessly. The CEO sees what genuinely needs them. Nothing else.

iv.

You are scaling, and the back office must scale faster.

Two consecutive International Property Awards. Active expansion into Dubai. A growing introducer network. Growth exposes operational weakness fastest. This role is to get ahead of that, not keep up with it. That is precisely the operator I am.

A Short Manifesto

So you know exactly what you're hiring.

Process matters. So does temperament. These are the principles I bring to the work — not aspirations, but operating habits.

i.

Confidentiality is binary.

Either everything is treated as confidential by default, or nothing meaningfully is. The CEO's inbox is a vault. Not a notebook.

ii.

Acknowledge before action.

If something needs an hour, I send a thirty-second acknowledgement first. Nobody should ever wonder if their message reached me.

iii.

Catch it before it ships.

The job is not to be busy. The job is to be the last set of eyes before something leaves the building. I take that seriously.

iv.

AI as co-pilot, never replacement.

I use modern tools daily — drafting, summarising, structuring messy data. Never unsupervised. Never confidential data in public tools.

v.

Document as I go.

Twice means a checklist. Five times means a template. The role I take on shouldn't be fragile. It should be transferable.

vi.

Surface problems early.

I'd rather flag at hour two that something will take six hours, than stay silent and miss the deadline at hour five.
